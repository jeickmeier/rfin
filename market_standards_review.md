## Valuations Market Standards Review

- **IRS float leg pricer misaligned with market conventions** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/irs/pricer.rs:330-403` builds a bespoke date grid and prices float coupons using accrual day count for forward-curve times, ignores `reset_lag_days`, and drops `fixing_calendar_id`. Resets therefore occur on payment dates with mismatched time basis to the forward curve. This diverges from standard IRS practice (T‑2 resets on fixing calendar using the forward curve’s own day count), shifting forwards, discount timing, and PV/greeks. *Fix:* drive the pricer off the shared cashflow builder so fixing dates use lag + fixing calendar, and compute forward times with the forward curve’s day count.

- **FRA reset/settlement conventions dropped** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/fra/types.rs:55-169` carries `reset_lag` but the pricer never uses it, nor any holiday calendar. It discounts from `as_of` to `start_date` directly and keeps projecting forwards even when the fixing date has passed (no observed fixing path). Market-standard FRA pricing uses fixing date = start_date − lag on a business-day calendar, settles on start, and switches to realized fixings once known. *Fix:* adjust fixing/value dates using lag + calendar, short-circuit to observed fixing when `as_of >= fixing_date`, and include settlement-day adjustments.

- **FX swap forward points inverted and missing spot-lag/cross-calendar logic** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/fx_swap/types.rs:200-208` computes `model_fwd = spot * DF_for / DF_dom`, which is the inverse of the Garman‑Kohlhagen/FX forward identity `F = S × DF_dom / DF_for`. Near-leg discount factors are omitted from the forward-point ratio, and no spot-lag or dual-calendar adjustment is applied to `near_date`/`far_date`. This produces systematically wrong forwards and PVs for non-flat curves and can flip carry direction. *Fix:* use `F = S × (DF_dom_far/DF_dom_near) / (DF_for_far/DF_for_near)` and build near/far dates from trade date using the proper joint spot calendar.

- **FX option convenience constructors hardwire USD curves for most pairs** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/fx_option/types.rs:108-152` falls back to `FxUnderlyingParams::new(base, quote, "USD-OIS", "USD-OIS")` for any pair other than EURUSD/GBPUSD. That applies USD discounting to both legs, ignoring domestic/foreign currencies, so forwards, premiums, and greeks are wrong for all other pairs. *Fix:* require explicit domestic/foreign curve ids for non-listed pairs or add per-currency defaults (e.g., JPY, CHF, CAD) instead of USD-for-all.

- **Equity underlying defaults ignore currency** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/common/parameters/underlying.rs:119-126` hardcodes `primary_curve_id` to `USD-OIS` for every equity underlying. Equity TRS, options, and exotics that lean on this default will discount/dividend-carry in USD even for non-USD underlyings. *Fix:* make the discount curve currency-aware (or require it) and propagate through equity-linked instruments.

- **Structured credit payments use weekend-only calendar placeholder** — `finstack/valuations/src/instruments/structured_credit/pricing/deterministic.rs:71-79` force-adjusts payment dates with a synthetic weekends-only calendar. Ignoring deal calendars (e.g., NYSE/TARGET) causes date shifts and accrued-interest errors, especially around holidays. *Fix:* carry a calendar id on the instrument and pass the resolved holiday calendar into the schedule builder.

- **Equity/FX exotics use instrument day count for curve/vol timing** — e.g., `barrier_option/pricer.rs:42-83` and `autocallable/pricer.rs:42-115` compute time-to-expiry with the instrument’s `day_count`, then query discount curves (`zero(t)`) and vol surfaces with that `t`, regardless of the curve/surface’s own basis. If users set non-standard day counts (252, Act/360) or the curves were built on a different basis, discounting and vols are skewed. *Fix:* derive `t` from the discount curve’s day count (for rates) and from the surface’s time convention, or validate that instrument `day_count` matches both before pricing.

- **FX swap/option workflows lack calendar-adjusted expiries** — Several FX constructs (e.g., `fx_option/types.rs` and `fx_swap/types.rs`) assume provided expiry/settlement dates are already business-adjusted; there is no joint USD/foreign calendar spot-roll or modified-following adjustment. Market-standard FX products roll trade date to spot on the joint calendar and adjust expiries/pay dates accordingly. *Fix:* add spot-date builder using both currency calendars and apply business-day conventions to expiries and settlements.
